Abstract

Transformer LLMs are bottlenecked by the KV cache, which scales linearly with context. Existing eviction methods score cache entries by their local contribution to the attention output, ignoring how that information propagates downstream to the final representation.

KVgrad instead assigns each entry a query-agnostic global importance score. A first-order Taylor expansion factorizes the impact of removing a cached value into a linear local component and a gradient-based downstream sensitivity, combined through a magnitude-aware, probe-specific scoring scheme. On RULER and LongBench, KVgrad reaches an average maximum compression of 6.42× with under 2% loss, and its signal distills cleanly into a lightweight MLP for zero-overhead online compression.

The idea

Look past the attention output

A cache entry's true importance isn't just what it contributes at its own layer — it's how far that contribution echoes through the remaining blocks. KVgrad measures the full influence flow from the value vector vcℓ to the final hidden state.

Prior work · local view

Scores the attention output only

KVzip and KVzip+ terminate the importance signal at layer ℓ, discarding everything the FFN and higher layers do with it.

KVgrad · global view

Traces influence to the final layer

A per-probe chain rule adds the downstream gradient sensitivity, capturing the entry's end-to-end effect in a single backward pass.
